About Unio Analytics

A career spent turning messy records
into numbers that hold.

Unio Analytics is a Vancouver practice operated by Jason Ahn, providing data and reporting support under the direction of lawyers, forensic accountants, and claims professionals.

Disputes and claims share one problem: the facts live in records nobody can read at a glance — thousands of punch-clock rows, years of payment exports, incident files written in a hurry. Before anyone can argue, opine, or settle, someone has to turn that material into organized, reconciled, defensible data. That is the entire job here.

Background

Jason's career has been a single thread through different rooms: taking large volumes of imperfect operational records and making them reliable enough for people with serious decisions to rely on.

That thread runs through analyst roles in Canadian federal intelligence and national police information management, IT security audit at a Big Four firm — including SOC 2 and ISO 27001 assessment work for financial institutions — and public-sector incident data analysis, where qualitative incident narratives are converted into structured, quantifiable reporting under formal review. He holds a criminology degree from Simon Fraser University.

The habits that define the work come from those rooms: every assumption written down, every data defect logged with the rule that resolved it, every figure traceable to its source — because in each of those environments, someone qualified eventually checks.

Specific employment history and dates are available on LinkedIn and verified on request.

Independence

Unio Analytics accepts civil and claims-related matters only, screens every engagement against a conflicts log, and declines matters connected to Jason's public-sector employment. Data support work is performed personally, on dedicated equipment, entirely outside that role.
